🛒 How to Buy Transit Tickets in 2026

Option 1: Navigo Easy Card (Easiest for Tourists)
Step 1: Buy Navigo Easy card (€2) at any metro ticket machine
Step 2: Load it with tickets or passes (Navigo Week, single tickets, etc.)
Step 3: Tap card on turnstile to enter
Where to buy: All metro stations, ticket machines have English option
Payment: Visa/Mastercard (NOT Amex)
Option 2: Mobile App (No Physical Card Needed)
Apps: "Bonjour RATP" or "Île-de-France Mobilités"
Download: iOS App Store or Google Play
Buy tickets: Directly in app with credit card
Validate: Hold phone to turnstile (NFC must be on)
Pro: Skip the ticket machine lines
Option 3: Navigo Découverte (For Weekly/Monthly Pass)
Cost: €5 one-time (same as Navigo Easy, but personalized)
Required: Passport photo (bring one or use photo booth at station)
Load: Weekly pass (€32.40) or monthly pass (€90.80)
Buy at: Gare du Nord ticket counter or machines
Your trip: This is what you need for Navigo Week

⚠️ Important Transit Tips

Metro vs Bus Tickets: They're SEPARATE! If you switch from metro to bus, you need TWO tickets (one metro €2.55, one bus €2.05). Only passes give you free transfers between networks.
RER B Pickpockets: RER B (airport line) is notorious for pickpockets. Keep bag in front, phone in pocket, don't fall asleep. Especially crowded during rush hours (8-9 AM, 5-7 PM).
Airport RER B Trains: Take ONLY B3 trains TO the airport (not B5). Check the display board shows "Aéroport CDG" lit up before boarding.
💡 Download Apps Before You Go: Citymapper (best for navigation), Bonjour RATP (official Paris transit), Google Maps offline, Uber. Make sure your phone plan has international data or buy SIM at airport.
